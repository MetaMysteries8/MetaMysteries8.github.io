# SM64 Speedrun-Informed Strategy (reference for the AI player)

This is **reference knowledge**, like a guide a human reads before playing. It is
NOT a list of inputs to replay. Use it to make smarter decisions from what you
SEE on screen. Play legitimately — no glitch abuse, no input playback.

## Legit vs. cheating (read this first)
- ✅ Legit: walking, running, jumping, long jumps, dives, swimming, wall-kicks,
  climbing, reading signs, grabbing — normal play you control from the screen.
- ⛔ Not for you: backwards long-jump (BLJ) infinite-speed glitches, clip/Out-of-
  Bounds tricks, save-state teleporting to skip content, or replaying a recorded
  run. These are off-limits — you must actually play.

## Core movement tech (how speedrunners move — do these legitimately)
- **Sustained running**: hold the stick fully toward your target. Real distance
  comes from *holding* a direction ~1–3 seconds, not tapping. This is the single
  biggest thing beginners (and weak AIs) get wrong.
- **Turn, then go**: Mario steers relative to the CAMERA. To reach something off
  to the side, briefly turn (Left/Right) to face it, THEN hold forward. A long
  turn just spins you in a circle.
- **Long jump** (big horizontal distance): run forward, then crouch+jump while
  moving. Great for crossing flat ground and gaps quickly. Don't long-jump toward
  ledges you might overshoot.
- **Dive** (action/C): a fast forward lunge; good for closing distance or grabbing.
  You slide on landing — don't dive off edges.
- **Triple jump**: three jumps in rhythm while running gives the highest jump —
  useful for tall ledges.
- **Wall-kick**: jump into a wall and jump again off it to gain height in corners.
- **Swimming**: you do NOT jump out of water. Hold a direction toward shore and
  tap jump (X) repeatedly — each tap is a stroke that pushes you forward and up.
  Aim for the nearest shallow edge and climb out.

## Camera
- Rotate the camera to face the way you want to travel before committing to a
  long run; it makes "forward" point where you actually want to go.
- If you can't see your target, pan the camera (look around) first.

## The early route (what to actually do at the start — you struggle here)
1. **Castle grounds (start area).** You spawn on a grassy area outside Peach's
   castle, near a moat and a bridge. The goal is the castle's front DOORS.
2. **Find the doors, not every arch.** The entrance is the pair of tall wooden
   DOORS set in the castle's front wall, across the bridge/path. Side archways
   and tunnels are NOT the entrance. Pick the doors as your target, face them,
   hold forward.
3. **Go inside.** Walk into the doors. Don't immediately run back outside.
4. **Advance any dialog** with jump (X) (the messages from the start / signs).
   Do NOT press Start during gameplay — that pauses.
5. **First level = Bob-omb Battlefield.** In the main foyer, the first room (no
   star required) holds the **Bob-omb Battlefield** painting (a grassy hill /
   mountain scene). Jump INTO the painting to enter the level.
6. **First star ("Big Bob-omb on the Summit").** Climb the mountain path to the
   top and reach the Big Bob-omb (King Bob-omb). Stay on the path, use the
   bridges, avoid the rolling balls and small bob-ombs.

## Level-goal mindset (general)
- Each level has 6 main stars + 100-coin star. Early game you only need a few
  stars to open the next areas, so prioritize the **easy, obvious** star.
- Most early stars are "get to the high/obvious place": climb to a summit, reach
  a clearly marked goal, or beat the level's boss. Follow the natural path upward.
- Coins and the red ❤ (health) help survive; grab them if convenient, don't
  detour far.

## Common failure modes (and the fix)
- **"I'm not moving."** You're holding forward into a wall, or facing the wrong
  way. TURN to a new direction or rotate the camera — don't keep mashing forward.
- **"I jumped to get out of water and nothing happened."** Jumping doesn't exit
  water. Hold toward shore + tap jump to swim out.
- **"I walked into an archway expecting the castle interior."** That's likely a
  side passage. Re-target the big front doors.
- **"The screen keeps changing on its own / Mario isn't responding."** You're
  probably on the TITLE SCREEN or an attract-mode DEMO. Press Start to leave the
  demo, then begin properly. Don't platform on a menu.
- **"I keep repeating the same move and getting nowhere."** Change something:
  new direction, back up, jump the obstacle, or pick another path.

## Speedrun categories (context only — you are NOT speedrunning for time)
- **0 Star**: reach Bowser and finish with no stars via heavy glitch routes.
  Off-limits for you (glitch-based) — listed only so you recognize the term.
- **1 Star / 16 Star / 70 Star / 120 Star**: collect that many stars before the
  final Bowser fight. Your job is just to make steady legitimate progress —
  reach the castle, enter levels, and collect the clearly-available stars.

Bottom line: **trust your eyes, face your target, hold the direction, and pick
the obvious next objective.** Speedrunners are just very good at those basics.
